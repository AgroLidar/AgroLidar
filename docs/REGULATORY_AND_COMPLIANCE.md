# Regulatory and Compliance

## 1. Applicable Frameworks (by market and integration scope)
- CE marking (Europe): Machinery Directive and Radio Equipment Directive when wireless components are present.
- FCC Part 15 (USA): wireless emissions for communication modules.
- ANATEL (Brazil): wireless component approval.
- ENACOM (Argentina): wireless component approval.
- ISO 25119: agricultural machinery functional safety (awareness reference).
- ISO 11783 (ISOBUS): agricultural communication standard (future integration alignment).

## 2. Current AgroLidar Status
AgroLidar is currently in prototype/pilot phase. Regulatory certification has not been obtained. Integrators are responsible for ensuring compliance with applicable regulations in their jurisdiction.

## 3. LiDAR and Hardware Compliance
Use sensor/compute manufacturer declarations and certifications for component-level evidence. Keep traceable BOM and revision records.

## 4. Data Privacy Considerations
- BEV frame data can be operationally sensitive.
- Location and machine telemetry may be regulated under local privacy frameworks.
- Define retention, access control, and incident response policies before scale-out.

## 5. Export Control Awareness
Cross-border movement of sensors, compute, and encryption-enabled modules may require export review.

## 6. Recommended Path to Production Certification
1. Freeze architecture and BOM.
2. Run hazard/risk analysis and safety case documentation.
3. Validate EMC/environmental/electrical compliance.
4. Validate software lifecycle and change control evidence.
5. Execute region-specific certification and labeling workflows.
